Loot Config DKP System Info FAQ

TNT Loot Configuration

A three-tiered loot distribution system based on DKP

What is it?

“TnT DKP” is our own take on DKP. It consists of two DKP phases: a “Priority” phase and a “Lottery” phase, followed by an Open Roll phase. Prior to the start of any given raid night, players designate which specific pieces of gear they desire from the Priority phase and Lottery phase by posting their “LootConfigs” in our DKP discord channel (this will eventually be done on our website).

How do I get DKP?

DKP is awarded as follows:

  • 25 DKP for on-time, taken at 7:00PM
  • 25 DKP for random AFK check at some point throughout the raid
  • 25 DKP for Lucifron, Gehennas, Geddon, Sulfuron, and Golemagg
  • 50 DKP for Magmadar, Garr, and Shazzrah
  • 100 DKP for Ragnaros
  • 75 DKP for Onyxia

We maintain two separate DKP tables for each player: one for Priority DKP and one for Lottery DKP. When DKP is awarded, it is awarded to both the Priority and Lottery DKP tables. Each player has a corresponding Priority DKP value and a Lottery DKP value. Priority and Lottery DKP is explained in the sections below.

Factoring in the weeks where there are two Onyxia raids, the total amount of DKP that each player can get per week on average should be roughly 500, if not a little more.

How much DKP do items cost?

All items in Phase 1 cost 1000 DKP, with the only exception being the Ancient Petrified Leaf and the Mature Black Dragon Sinew, which cost 500 each (totaling 1000). The reasoning behind this cost is so that it should take roughly two raid weeks before a player can afford their first piece of loot at Priority.

What is the “Priority” phase?

When an item drops, it first goes through the Priority phase. Priority DKP operates exactly the same as classic DKP. The winner of this item is simply the player with the highest Priority DKP who has this item in their Priority Config. When the winner is determined, the item is looted to that player and the appropriate amount of Priority DKP is deducted immediately.

What is the “Lottery” phase?

The Lottery phase is completely unique to our loot system. When an item is being distributed, if no players had that item in their Priority Config, a check is then done to see who has this item in their Lottery Config. For all players who have this item in their Lottery Config, a “Lottery” is initiated as such:

  • Each player receives a number of “tickets” equal to their Lottery DKP (with a minimum of 1)
  • Each player has a “range” assigned to them automatically by the AddOn
  • The AddOn then executes a random roll from 1 to the total number of tickets in the Lottery
  • The result of the roll is then checked against the designated “ranges” for each player to determine who wins
  • The actual Lottery logic is executed entirely by the AddOn, including the roll. When the winner is determined, the item is looted to the winner and the appropriate amount of Lottery DKP is deducted immediately.

What items can I place in my Loot Config?

Both the Priority and Lottery phases are intended to be avenues to gear our raiders with main spec loot. We follow a basic intuition here regarding a sense of “main spec”. A Druid will be either Feral or Resto. A Paladin will be either Ret or Holy. A Priest will be either Shadow or “Healing” (we don’t differentiate between Holy and Disc). Etc. As such, it is left to the Officers/class leads to define what is and is not acceptable for their players to place their Priority and Lottery lists.

One caveat to this is PvP gear. We allow our raiders to gear themselves for PvP via the Lottery system.As such, a player is allowed to place any items that they will be using for active PvP in their Lottery configs (as well as any main spec PvE gear, of course). Again, full discretion is left to the Officers/class leads to overrule any loot designations if needed. It’s important to clarify that even though we are allowing our raiders to pick up PvP gear via the Lottery system, Lottery is not an avenue by which players can get PvE offspec loot.

What if I PvP as a spec other than the one I raid as?

If a player mainly PvPs as a spec that differs from their PvE raiding spec, then it is completely within their right to place items for their PvP spec on their Lottery. For example, if one of our Paladins only PvPs as Ret, then that player is completely within his/her right to place Spinal Reaper or Bonereaver’s Edge (or any other 2H) in their Lottery config, as well as other Melee DPS items.

What happens if no one has the item on either Priority or Lottery?

If no one has a particular item on either Priority or Lottery, then it simply goes to Open Roll, where our only requirement is “you must plan on using it at some point”. By “using it” we don’t necessarily mean in raid: you could intend to use the item to farm 5man content, or use it when your soloing in the world, or use it when PvPing – we don’t care, as long as you’re not going to DE or vendor it. This is the avenue by which players may obtain offspec loot.

Can DKP go negative?

Yes, both Priority DPK and Lottery DKP can go negative, but going negative in each has different meaning.

In Lottery, going negative means that you will be reduced to a single ticket in the lottery. This does not mean that your Lottery DKP has a floor of 1, however. Your Lottery DKP can and will continue to go deeper and deeper into the negatives if you continue to win items with your 1 Lottery ticket. All this means is that it’ll take you longer to crawl out of your hole to be able to have more than 1 ticket in future lotteries.

In Priority, we actually enforce a rule whereby players cannot win items at Priority if they don’t have enough DKP to afford the cost up-front. However, for any such players who can’t afford the item but have it in their Priority config, when that item drops we go ahead and add the item to their Lottery Configs automatically (the AddOn handles this in real-time). This way, if only one player had this item on their Priority Config and they couldn’t afford it, if and when it moves onto the Lottery phase the player still has a shot at it. This change to the player’s Lottery Config is temporary, and will only be in affect for the current raid night.

Given the above rule, it shouldn’t be possible for a player to end up in the negatives in Priority DKP. However, there are certain edge cases that could cause it, such as if the player is a designated recipient of a legendary – they are going to receive that item regardless and be charged its cost regardless.

In what order are the items from a boss distributed?

Items are always distributed in ItemID order, ascending. The order in which items are distributed matters can drastically change the outcome of who gets what. If this isn’t obvious to you, consider the scenario where you and another Warrior have Quick Strike Ring(ItemID 18821) and Obsidian Edged Blade(ItemID 18822) in your Priority Configs. You currently have 100 more Priority DKP than the other Warrior. We kill Magmadar and both items drop. Given our ItemID ascending distribution rule, we would distribute the ring first, resulting in you winning the ring and having 1000 Priority DKP deducted. Then, we would distribute the Obsidian Edged Blade, resulting in the other Warrior getting the sword as he now has higher Priority DKP than you. If, however, we had distributed the Obsidian Edged Blade first, the results would have been flip-flopped, and you would have received the sword. If the officer running the Master Looting for that raid night was particularly fond of you and knew that you really wanted the sword, maybe he/she would have opted to distribute the sword first just to ensure you got it. It’s important to us that we don’t introduce any bias like this into our loot system, so we choose to enforce consistency via ItemID.

Can I place Eye of Sulfuras in my Loot Config?

No. In our guild, legendary items will always be decided via loot council. However, designated recipients will still be charged the standard cost of an item: 1000 Priority DKP for Eye of Sulfuras, 500 Priority DKP for each of the two bindings. More information regarding the selection process and guild resourcing for legendaries will be posted on a separate page at a later date.

Can I update my Loot Config during the raid?

No, with one exception. Loot Config modifications close at 5:00PM PST on raid nights. Any changes that come in after 5:00PM are disregarded. There is one exception to this rule, however. The exception is if you have two items in your Loot Config for the same slot, where winning one might make you no longer desire the other. An example of this is Mana Igniting Cord and Sash of Whispered Secrets for Shadow Priests/Warlocks.

Let’s say you are a Warlock and you have both Mana Igniting Cord and Sash of Whispered Secrets in your Priority Config. Let’s also say that you have 3000 Priority DKP and the next highest Warlock/SPriest only has 1500 Priority DKP. We kill Baron Geddon, Mana Igniting Cord drops, and you win it and get charged 1000 Priority DKP. The raid moves along and kills Domo. Domo now drops Sash of Whispered Secrets. Well, you still have Sash of Whispered Secrets on your Priority Config and you still have more Priority DKP than the other players with it in their Priority Config, so the AddOn determines that you should win it. Ideally, you should be able to inform the Officers so that you could have removed the Sash of Whispered Secrets from your Priority list upon receiving the Mana Igniting Cord to prevent gear going to waste.

To avoid this situation, the Master Looter who is running our TnTDKP AddOn can update players’ Loot Configs during the raid for this one specific circumstance. To do this, a player would need to whisper an Officer (any officer will do) immediately after receiving the initial piece of gear. In the above example, this would mean that once the Warlock received the Mana Igniting Cord, he/she would have to immediately reach out to an Officer to request removing Sash of Whispered Secrets from his/her Priority Config.

What is the distinction between “Core” and “Reserve” raiders?

There are two distinctions between Core and Reserve raiders. The first is straightforward: being a Core Raider means that you have a guaranteed raid invite.

The second distinction is when Priority loot is being distributed. Reserve raiders cannot take items via Priority DKP over Core raiders who have those items also in their Priority Configs, regardless of whether or not the Reserve raider has higher Priority DKP than the Core raider. This only affects Priority items. Core and Reserve raiders Lottery against each other just the same, and Reserve raiders can still win items at Priority if there are no Core raiders with this item in their Priority Configs.

Will there be any DKP decay?

DKP “Decay” is a tool used by guilds to control the inflation of DKP. This is useful for making sure that newcomers can come in and have a reasonable shot at loot. We plan on doing DKP decay between major raid tiers. At the present time, that means that we will be doing a DKP decay prior to the first BWL raid. Decay will be done as a %, but the specific value will be determined as we draw closer to BWL’s release. Our general stance on Decay between raid tiers is to do an extreme decay, effectively fully resetting the DKP to put everyone on roughly the same page, while preserving the same order of who was on top at the conclusion of the previous raid tier.
